Fiona Bruce says sexualisation of young girls is her 'biggest concern'

Fiona Bruce has said that the sexualisation of young girls is her "biggest concern" as a feminist.

Television presenter Fiona Bruce Photo: REX FEATURES
8:00AM GMT 06 Mar 2010

Comments

The television presenter said she thought that the situation had got worse in recent years with the increasing number of sexual images surrounding children.

The Antiques Roadshow host, who is married to Nigel Sharrocks, a media executive, and has two children of her own, said: "It's my biggest concern as a feminist.

"I work in a lookist industry being in television but there seems to be so many overtly sexual images of women now and it puts so much pressure on young girls. “

The newsreader, who was ranked at 98 in a list of the "100 Sexiest women" in the men’s magazine FHM last year, added: "Whatever happened to the top shelf in newsagents? Whenever I go into my corner shop now there are just breasts everywhere.

"Maybe I'm just old but I definitely think it's a huge problem."

Her comments follow a report by psychologist Dr Linda Papadopoulos, which warned about the “pornification” of society in which girls and boys are put under unprecedented pressure to conform to gender stereotypes from a younger and younger age.

It said girls are pressured into appearing sexually available while boys are forced to appear macho and to think about women as sexual objects.

The report said magazines aimed at young men contributed to the problem.

Miss Bruce also spoke about the idea of making the salaries of BBC presenters public – a move which she would support.

"I try to ignore everyone probing me about it but the interest in my salary is baffling", she said.

"I've noticed my apparent earnings fluctuate from one newspaper page to the next. For the record, if the BBC did decide to publish my earnings I would be absolutely fine with that. It's the public's money - you are the ones paying. You have a right to know."
