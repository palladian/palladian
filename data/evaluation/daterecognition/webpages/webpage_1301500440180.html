Yanzhou Coal Breaks Out As Coal Stocks Climb

By DONALD H. GOLD, INVESTOR'S BUSINESS DAILY Posted 03/17/2011 06:15 PM ET

Featured Stocks

  • BTU *
    Peabody Energy Corp
    ICO *
    I C G Inc (Added 03/24/2011)
    PUDA
    Puda Coal Inc
  • WLT *
    Walter Energy Inc (Added 03/07/2011)
    YZC *
    Yanzhou Coal Mining Adr (Added 03/24/2011)
* Top-Rated Company
1" 2"

Why are coal stocks holding up so well during this broad market correction? Somehow, producers of this ancient form of energy found themselves in the No. 18 slot out of the 197 industries tracked by IBD as of Thursday's edition.

The best-rated foreign issue in this group, Yanzhou Coal Mining (YZC), broke out from a cup-with-handle base on Thursday. The 32.09 buy point triggered a 67% surge in volume.

Yanzhou's handle is flawed. It contains a high-volume downside gap, though much of the chart contains price gaps. The best handles are tight, quiet and low in volume — as if nobody's watching.

 View Enlarged Image

The biggest risk is that the stock broke out amid a broad market correction.

This is the time when new buys should be pretty much off the table. While some stocks will rise in the face of falling averages, most won't.

Corrections are typically full of failed breakouts, broken uptrends and busts below the 10-week moving average.

Still, by braving the head winds of a bearish market, Yanzhou deserves attention.

Yanzhou has shown wide-swinging EPS results in recent quarters. EPS surged 186% in Q1 2010, fell 54% in Q2 and rose 229% in Q3.

But sales growth has accelerated for three straight quarters, with a 67% surge in the most recent quarter.

Yanzhou logged a 39% after-tax margin in Q4, its highest in at least four years. That's the impact on Yanzhou's bottom line of rising coal prices.

And they have been rising. Australian thermal coal has climbed 36% in the year through February.

Yanzhou trades just 262,000 ADRs on an average day in the U.S. But its Shanghai and Hong Kong listings each trade more than 12 million shares.

Yanzhou Coal is one of a number of positive-looking coal stocks. Other attractive names, based in the U.S. or abroad, include Walter Energy (WLT), Peabody Energy (BTU), ICG (ICO) and Puda Coal (PUDA).

That last name is the only other highly rated coal stock from China. But its earnings results are faltering, and the stock is trading below its 10-week moving average after a 43% correction.

Even after rising 900% since March 2009, Puda is still a low-priced stock, trading around 11 a share.

Is Japan's calamity driving coal stocks? In part, yes. But a sudden global rush to coal amid a shuttering of nuclear power plants doesn't seem likely.

More likely, some analysts say, is that a second look at nuclear will prevent a shift away from coal.

Remember also that Australia's producers have yet to fully recover from recent flooding. Mines and rails were damaged enough to affect supply, much of which would normally flow to China.

Also, with crude trading above $100 a barrel, coal looks that much better. Coal may also be enjoying special focus because crude is not just expensive; it's in short supply in Europe and Asia.
