    England v Pakistan commentary

    Last updated: 7th June 2009  

    England (185-5) beat Pakistan (137-7) by 48 runs Well, that was unexpected. Not just the victory, but the manner of it. England batted pretty well, then bowled and fielded superbly, but Pakistan were absolutely dreadful. They were slipshod in the field and completely off the pace with the bat. The lack of urgency was baffling, and England have cruised home. England are through to the Super Eight - and Pakistan are now the team under pressure. They not only need to beat Holland, they need to do so convincingly in order to overturn a huge gulf in the NRR calculations.

    Over 20 Anderson 4-0-0-0-2-2 137-7 - Anderson to bowl the final over, a mere formality. Umar Gul is the new batsman, and he has a bit of fun by smacking a four through the covers. He can't lay a bat on the next three, but a miscued drive brings him two more. The last delivery coming up... and it's hit away for two through midwicket. England have thrashed Pakistan by 48 runs and got themselves out of jail after that horror show against the Netherlands.

    Over 19 Broad 0-1-1-0-2-0 129-7 - Some Pakistan fans are filing out of the ground now. I'd imagine they're as baffled as I am by this weak performance from their side. Broad into the attack and, just for laughs, we've got a runner involved as well now. Looks like Arafat is the man in need of assistance. The batsmen are even letting the ball go through to the keeper now. Curious. I tell you what, Pakistan v Holland is going to be a huge game now. There'll be a game within a game there, because Pakistan are going to have to win that game by a long way to recover the Net Run-Rate deficit they'll face after this. WICKET! Arafat and his runner depart as he slaps the ball straight to Wright at long-off. "We can see you sneaking out," announce the delighted England fans.

    Over 18 Anderson W-2-1-1-0-4 125-6 WICKET! Anderson gets a deserved wicket as he comes back into the attack. Misbah tries to clear long-on and doesn't manage it, Morgan collecting his second catch of the innings. Well, at least Misbah tried to give it some tap. Younus chips down the track for two and whacks the last ball of the over for four. Not that it matters - that still sends the required rate climbing.

    Over 17 Wright 0-1-1-6-0-1 117-5 - Misbah - perhaps the most dangerous batsman in the Pakistan side - only appeared in the middle in the 17th over. Things could have been very different if he'd been here earlier, and he shows that by smashing the first six of the innings over midwicket. Drop! A rare blemish from England as Misbah slices the ball tamely to Bopara at backward-point, but he barely lays a hand on it. He gestures that it came out of the lights, and he and Wright are able to smile about it. They wouldn't have been smiling if it was 30 to win off three instead of 70.

    Over 16 Swann W-1-1-2-1-1 108-5 WICKET! Afridi's tortured innings comes to an end. He finally gets a decent chunk of bat on the ball, but can only pick out the sub fielder Eoin Morgan at long-on. He takes the catch, and the task gets harder and harder for the 2007 runners-up. Younus sweeps a single, and new batsman Misbah adds one more to get off the mark. Younus picks up two with a clip over midwicket, but the required rate is now up at almost 20 runs per over. England have bowled and fielded brilliantly, but this has been a bizarre batting effort from Pakistan. Just 10 fours. No sixes. What's the gameplan?

    Over 15 Wright 0-0-1-2-2-4 102-4 - "Afridi," says Ian Chappell, "is living on reputation at the moment." You can see what he means. He's struggling to just get bat on ball here and gets a single to third-man to move on to five off 11 balls. Younus gets two to deep point from the next ball, but the required rate is almost three a ball. A superb sliding stop from Anderson keeps Younus down to two again from a powerful drive. England have been far better in the field today: compared to their own efforts the other night and Pakistan's today. Younus, who's been the one batsman to show some sense of the necessary urgency, clubs the last ball of the over through midwicket for four. But it's surely too late. Hundred's up.

    Over 14 Rashid 1-0-1-1-0-1 93-4 - Rashid to bowl his final over. It's been a good spell so far - can he finish it off. Just one run from the first two balls is a fine start before Afridi gets a big top-edge that somehow lands safe as Foster and two fielders converge on it. Younus slices the next one to deep point for a single - almost carried to Mascarenhas - and Afridi can only add one more run from the final ball of the over. Superb stuff from the young legspinner, who completes his four overs for just 24 runs.

    Over 13 Wright 0wd-2-0wd-W-0-0-2-0 89-4 - Wright into the attack, and he's ourtrageously wided by umpire Harper for a ball that Malik almost edges through to the keeper. A thick edge to third-man brings two more before Harper gives another scandalous wide for a bouncer that whistles past the batsman's ears. If Harper had his way, one suspects there would just be a bowling machine serving up length balls for the batsmen to smack out of the park. WICKET! This one's not a wide, and this time Malik does get an edge on the ball and Foster completes the formalities. Afridi is the new batsman, and Pakistan need some Boom Boom tonight. He drills his first ball at cover, where Collingwood makes a superb diving stop. A swing and a miss follows, and the pressure mounts. Afridi just clears cover with the next one and picks up two runs, but it's an excellent over from Wright: just six runs and a wicket coming from it. The required rate climbs to 14.

    Over 12 Swann 1-1-1-4-1-1 83-3 - "Big shot coming now," yells Foster as the first three balls bring only singles. He's right as well, as Younus smacks the next ball straight down the ground and beats long-off. Foster anticipates the reverse-sweep next ball and gets some pad on it. Probably saved a boundary there. Even with the boundary, that's an OK over for England. Nine came from it, but the required rate's almost 13.

    Over 11 Rashid 4-1-4-1lb-0-1 74-3 - Malik gets a much-needed boundary with a powerful sweep shot and Younus repeats the trick two balls later. That's more like it from the men in green. There's a chance for a run-out from the next ball with Younus slow to set off for a single, but the throw at the bowler's end from Bopara is wild. Rashid ends the over quite well. After nine from the first three balls, there's just a leg-bye and a single from the last three. Still not a single maximum in this innings from Pakistan.

    Over 10 Swann 1-2-1-0-1-0 63-3 - The required rate touches 12 for the first time, and this is now getting serious for Pakistan. We're reaching the point of no return - one of these batsmen has to tee off and do it now or the power hitters like Shahid Afridi, Misbah-ul-Haq and Yasir Arafat are simply going to have too much to do.

    Over 9 Rashid 2-0-0-1-0-2 58-3 - Malik gets two with a deft glance behind point, but two dot balls after that get huge cheers from the crowd. A flipper from Rashid there as well. Malik manages to get a single to deep point from the next ball, but Younus misses an attempted slog-sweep and has to be careful to keep his foot grounded as Foster whips off the bails. Two come through point from the final ball, but it's another excellent over from Rashid. Pakistan slipping further behind the rate here, and they're 30 runs behind where England were at this stage of the innings.

    Over 8 Swann 1-0-4-0-1-1 53-3 - Spin at both ends now as Swann comes into the attack. Younus gets a much-needed boundary with a powerful sweep shot that a diving Wright can't quite save on the square-leg boundary.

    Over 7 Rashid 0-1-1-1-0-2 46-3 - The Pakistan fans are quieter than they've been all evening as Adil Rashid comes into the attack with the fielding restrictions relaxed. It's a good time for him to sneak some cheap overs through with Pakistan forced to rebuild. Just five runs from the over. And, to add some spice to proceedings, it's starting to spit with rain. Pakistan are miles behind on D/L.

    Over 6 Broad 1-0-1-2-W-W 41-3 - Akmal pulls to deep square-leg for a single. Butt tries to smash a drive down the ground but hits it straight to Anderson at mid-off. For what it's worth, Pakistan were behind on D/L at the five-over stage. Good bowling this from Broad, as another very full delivery is prodded into the offside for a single. Another Akmal pull shot brings two runs, but Pakistan, so far, have the English problem of being unable to find the boundary. WICKET! And that pressure tells in the end. Akmal goes for the pull once more, but gets too much elevation and picks out Wright on the fence. He takes the catch, using his hands this time instead of the armpit method he favoured against Holland, and England have a second big wicket inside the powerplay. Shoaib Malik is the new batsman, but he won't be on strike. WICKET! Two in two for Broad as Butt takes on the short ball and can only sky the ball tamely to Bopara at backward-point. England all over Pakistan here, and Broad will be on a hat-trick at the start of his next over. Younus Khan is the new batsman, and he arrives with his team in trouble.

    Over 5 Anderson 4-0-1-0-0-3 37-1 - Anderson changes ends after a good two-over spell from Mascarenhas. Butt hits the first ball of the over across the square on the legside, and unless it's going straight to a fielder that always means boundary. A single from the third ball of the over finally brings Akmal on strike for the first time, while there's another delay while a broken bail is replaced. Bad day for the wickets so far. Akmal has a huge mow at his first delivery but misses. He's more defensive next time round, but the result's the same as the ball whistles through to Foster. Akmal finally gets some bat on ball with another almighty swing at the final ball of the over, and manages to just about slice the ball over the cover fielders. It skips away across the square, but there was no timing at all in the shot and Collingwood is able to just reel the ball in and save a run. Five overs completed, so we've got a match. That's one problem solved for England.

    Over 4 Broad 0-4-0-0-4-1 29-1 - Every ball being cheered to the rafters now. Stuart Broad's into the attack after just one over from Anderson, and his first ball is hit straight at point. No run there, and the England fans cheer. The next ball is played just behind point, and a diving Wright can't get there. The ball runs away for four, and it's the Pakistan fans in full cry. Can't recall many better atmospheres at an English ground for a one-day game. Sensational. A swing and a miss followed by a miscued pull to mid-on build the pressure on Butt, but Broad offers a gift with a short, wide ball outside the off stump and it's smeared over point for four. Butt again keeps the strike with a single from the final ball. Still poor old Akmal waits his turn...

    Over 3 Mascarenhas W-2-0-4-0-1 20-1 WICKET! Shehzad tries to go over the top but slices his drive high into the sky, and Collingwood judges the catch nicely moving back from mid-off. Butt is down the track to carve the next one over point for two, and that prompts Foster to come up to the stumps. Butt goes over the top with more success than Shehzad - despite the bottom hand coming off the bat - and picks up a one-bounce four over mid-off. A swipe into the legside brings Butt a single from the last ball of the over, so he'll keep the strike. New batsman Kamran Akmal has to wait for his chance.

    Over 2 Anderson 0-0wd-1-0wd-0-0-0wd-1-1 13-0 - Jimmy Anderson's first delivery shapes away and beats Shehzad all ends up. A wide down the legside is followed by a clip to long-leg for a single. Mascarenhas got away with a wide in his last over, but Billy Doctrove has levelled it up here. Butt backs away, so Anderson pushes the ball across him. Looks wide because of where the batsman was, but you've got to give the bowler something even in Twenty20 cricket. Another harsh-looking wide outside the off stump prompts Pietersen and Collingwood to go to the umpire to check whether it was for line or height. Either way it was harsh. Three wides from the over, but only three other singles so it's a good over for England.

    Over 1 Mascarenhas 1-0-1-0-1-4 7-0 - Collingwood throws the ball to Mascarenhas straight away. Ahmed Shehzad works the first ball to leg for a single, before Mascarenhas gets away with a wide outside the off stump. The fans in green inside The Oval make their feelings known. Butt hits the next ball out to deep point for a single before a good off-cutter has Shehzad in trouble as it jags back and hits the thigh-pad. A scampered single follows, and Mascarenhas spoils a good opening over with a wide half-volley to end it. Butt leans into a classic cover-drive and collects the first boundary of the innings. Bedlam in the stands as the crowd goes wild for that shot.

    England 185-5 Well that's certainly better from England. Pietersen showed just why England were so desperate to get him back in the side, while the rest of the batsman played with far more intent than in the Holland game. A handy partnership at the end got England up past 180, but have they learned their lessons too late to stay in the tournament? Should be a close finish here.

    Over 20 Umar Gul 1-1-1-2-1-2 185-5 - Excellent start to the final over from Umar as two yorkers produce just two singles. Another excellent yorker is blocked back to the bowler, but he has an attack of the Stuart Broad's and throws wildly at the stumps. England scamper an overthrow. Drop! Another chance goes down as Salman Butt drops Foster at deep cover. He then compounds the error by fumbling around as he tries to pick the ball up, and the batsmen complete a second run. Another pinpoint yorker is dug out for a single by Foster, before Umar does another Broad impression to turn a scampered single into two from the final ball of the innings. Decent total from England, should be a fascinating run-chase.

    Over 19 Afridi 4-2-0-1-6-2 177-5 - Afridi to bowl his final over. He starts the over with a long-hop, and Mascarenhas can't believe his luck as he pulls it through square-leg for four. The next ball is heaved over midwicket for two, althrough Mascarenhas was on the treadmill for a while trying to get back for the second. The next ball is 79mph, and unsurprisingly Mascarenhas misses it and Akmal fails to gather cleanly. What a change of pace that is. Mascarenhas gets a single from the next ball before Foster hits an astonishing six, smacked over long-off off the back foot. Bizarre shot, but so effective. Two scampered runs complete an excellent over for England - 15 runs from it. They've got a competitive total, but it's certainly not a position of safety.

    Over 18 Arafat 1-0-1-1-1-1 162-5 - Mascarenhas scrambles a single from the first ball of the over as Arafat comes back into the attack. A swing and a miss from Foster produces a priceless dot ball, and the England keeper would surely be better off getting Mascarenhas back on strike. Drop! He does so, but he should be gone. Foster paddles the ball to short fine-leg where Saeed, moving forward, fails to hold on to a reasonably straightforward chance. Mascarenhas can only manage a single from the next one, and England are in real danger of once again failing to turn a decent start into an imposing total. Foster hits an excellent yorker down the ground for a single, and Mascarenhas can't do any better from the final ball. Superb over from Arafat as the Pakistan comeback/England slump continues.

    Over 17 Saeed Ajmal W-1-1-2-W-1 157-5 WICKET! Paul Collingwood tries to clear Afridi at long-off and doesn't manage it. Simple catch, and easily taken. That brings England's big-hitter - "Favourite shot: any six" Mascarenhas to the crease. He's almost run out without facing as Pietersen calls him through for a single, but he gets home safely in the end. Direct hit would have been a different single. Mascarenhas gets off the mark with a single before Pietersen picks up two with a well-weighted clip into the legside. WICKET! Massive wicket, and this could cost England dear. Pietersen top-edges a slog-sweep and is caught at short third-man. Pietersen departs for a superb 58, but England have lost two key wickets in the over. England, remember, failed to find a single boundary in their last 17 deliveries against Holland: a repeat of that will leave England with a well below-par total. James Foster is the new batsman, at the non-striker's end. Mascarenhas takes a single from the final ball, but it's a quite brilliant over from the young doosra bowler who ends his spell with sensational figures of 2-23. Brilliant.

    Over 16 Umar Gul 2-4-4-1-1-1lb 152-3 - Drop! Collingwood chips over square-leg, and Yasir Arafat running in from the rope can't get there to complete the catch. Two great shots through midwicket - one off the back foot, one off the front - bring Collingwood much-needed boundaries and he brings up the 150 with a single to point. England needed a big over, and they've had it here. Eleven from the first four balls. An excellent yorker from Umar is dug out by Pietersen, and the batsmen scamper a single. Collingwood tries to smear the last ball of the over into the legside but misses and has to settle for a leg-bye.

    Over 15 Saeed Ajmal 1-0-1-0-1-0 139-3 - Saeed Ajmal back into the attack. This is a gamble. Collingwood takes a single from the first ball, but Pietersen misses the next one. Pietersen comes down the track to the next one, but the bowler sees him coming and spears in a dart that KP can only bunt down the ground for a single. Collingwood aims over midwicket next ball but misses, and it didn't miss the off stump by much. Just a single from the next ball, and Pietersen can only defend the final ball back to the bowler. Bringing the young spinner back on at this stage was a major risk, but he's repaid the faith in fine style. Just three singles from the over.

    Over 14 Umar Gul 0-W-1-0wd-1lb-1-4 136-3 - Umar is back into the attack, and starts with a clever slower ball that Shah can only defend back to the bowler. WICKET! The next one's even better as Shah misses a straight one and departs for 33 off 32 balls. England play safe, sending Paul Collingwood in as scheduled rather than Dimi Mascarenhas, and a new stump also comes out at the fall of that wicket because the leg stump was snapped in two. Blimey. Collingwood does the right thing and gets a single to turn the strike back over to Pietersen. Umar Gul goes for the bouncer, but it sails way over Pietersen's head and is quite rightly signalled wide. The next ball is turned into the legside and the batsmen complete a single. There are cheers from the crowd believing Pietersen has completed his half-century, but umpire Daryl Harper signals leg-bye. I've never heard boos like that greet a leg-bye before. A thin inside edge from Pietersen beats the leg-stump and runs away to the boundary. England needed that boundary, and now the crowd can stand to hail a fine 50 (31 balls, 5x4s 3x6s).

    Over 13 Aamir 2-0-6-1-0-6 128-2 - Aamir is back into the attack. Shah clips the first ball over midwicket and thinks it's going for four. It isn't, and Shah has to get his skates on to get back for the second run. A swing and a miss from Shah proves to be only a practice swing as he launches the next one high over midwicket and over the longest boundary on the ground. A slower ball from Aamir is defended into the offside for a single, and Pietersen steps away and smacks a length delivery over wide long-off for the second maximum in the over. This is much more like it from England, with Pietersen now on 49.

    Over 12 Afridi 0-4-1-6-1-1 113-2 - Shah finally gets Afridi away with another powerful sweep shot that beats long-leg's dive. Pietersen goes for the sweep and gets a big top-edge on it. But it sails towards the shortest boundary on the ground and carries for six easily. Good over for England, who are in a decent position here. The 50 partnership comes up.

    Over 11 Saeed Ajmal 4-1-4-0-1-0 100-2 - Glorious shot from Pietersen, driving in-to-out and over extra-cover for four. England can't get Afridi away, so they've got to go for it at the other end. Pietersen sweeps hard for a single, before Shah plays a similar shot but places it between the two boundary riders and picks up four. The hundred comes up - at almost exactly the same point as against Holland on Friday - with a single. Can England kick on this time?

    Over 10 Afridi 0-0-0-0-0-0 90-2 - Shah having a terrible time against Afridi here. He's almost bowled off the first ball trying to sweep, then almost gets run out from the next one looking for a non-existent single before bottom-edging the next one past the stumps and through the keeper's legs for a single. Pietersen adds another single with a firm sweep to deep square-leg - where Aamir almost runs past the ball before sticking out a hand and making the save. Excellent over from Afridi, and the last three overs have cost just 14 runs.

    Over 9 Saeed Ajmal 2-0-0-2-1lb-0wd-0 88-2 - Saeed Ajmal into the attack, and Shah places him cleverly into the legside for a couple of twos. It's a big boundary out there, and anything that just gets out of the 30-yard circle is always two. There's a leg-bye from the next delivery, but the chance of an overthrow is lost as Shah loses his footing trying to turn. Drop! A wide down the legside is followed by a powerful drive from Pietersen that Saeed cannot hold on to in his followthrough. Tough chance, but it really should have been taken.

    Over 8 Afridi 1-1-1-1-1-1 82-2 - Afridi into the attack, so it's spin at both ends. Good start for the unorthodox leggie, with England having to deal in singles.

    Over 7 Malik 0wd-4-1-0nb-4-0-1-1 76-2 - With the fielding restrictions lifted, it's spin straight away through Shoaib Malik. He starts with a horrible wide down the legside, and the second is swept hard through square-leg by Pietersen. Two men out on the fence out there, but neither of them can get anywhere near it. A single brings Shah on strike, and a no-ball from Malik allows the Middlesex man to get his innings up and running off a free hit. He smacks a full-toss - doubly generous - over mid-off for a one-bounce four, and there's certainly nothing wrong with England's run-rate at the start of this innings - albeit assisted by some pretty ordinary bowling and fielding.

    Over 6 Umar Gul 1-4nb-3-W-0wd-0-0-0 63-2 - Umar Gul into the attack after his fielding blooper in the last over. His first ball is clipped away to deep square-leg for a single by Wright. The next ball is a no-ball and Pietersen clubs it down the ground for four more. He's now got a free hit to go after as well, and Pietersen hits it to point where another misfield turns one run into three. WICKET! Umar gets it right at least with a searing yorker that beats Wright for pace and makes a horrible mess of the stumps. Still, Wright has given this innings some momentum with 34 off 16 balls. Owais Shah is the new batsman for England, and the first ball he faces is a wide down the legside. Now it's the England fans cheering again. Slip in place for the new batsman - good aggression from Younus. Another excellent yorker is just about kept out by Shah and, after a terrible start, Umar Gul has dragged that over back pretty well. An entertaining first six overs, with the crowd getting louder with each passing delivery.

    Over 5 Arafat 0-1-4-4-1-4nb-6 53-1 - Pietersen, watchful thus far, hits the first ball of the over into the ground and back to the bowler. Arafat 'catches' it, and the crowd goes wild. A push to the left of mid-off brings a single, before Wright muscles a short ball into the gap between midwicket and mid-on for four. Wright is down the track again to the next ball and gets a thick edge to third-man where Umar Gul gets in to a beautiful long-barrier position... and lets the ball bounce through his legs and away for four. He sees the funny side - as does his captain - but the bowler looks less amused. The England fans behind Umar enjoyed it too. Wright scampers a clever quick single from the next ball before Arafat bowls a high full-toss that Pietersen flat-bats baseball-style through mid-on for four. It's given as a no-ball, and Pietersen cashes in on the extra delivery with the second biggest six we've hit here. It's way up in the top tier straight back over the bowler's head and is clocked at 104m. A couple of sensational overs for England have got them up and running in fine style now.

    Over 4 Aamir 0-1-0-4-6-4 32-1 - Pietersen pulls a short ball from Aamir hard into the ground but straight to the fielder at mid-on, and picks up a single to mid-off from the next delivery. Wright gives Aamir the charge next ball, but the 17-year-old bangs it in short at good pace - 88mph - and Wright makes no contact. The ball flies through to Akmal and the crowd roar again. Sensational atmosphere, it really is. Wright takes on the next one and sends it high over mid-on for a one-bounce four. We've seen some massive hits in this tournament, but there haven't been many that went higher than that. It was almost in orbit. The next ball is short, but Wright is ready for it and - glory be - smashes it over midwicket for England's first six of the tournament. An outside edge from the last ball of the over brings Wright four streaky runs, but boy did England need that injection of pace.

    Over 3 Arafat 0-1-0-2-0-4 17-1 - Pietersen drives the first ball of the over hard but straight to mid-off and takes on the mid-on fielder from the next ball. Had Saeed fielded cleanly, it might have been tight. Wright shuffles across his stumps and plays the ball through the pigeons at midwicket for two runs. Arafat spoils a good over with a leg-stump half-volley that Wright easily - and gratefully - clips past short fine-leg and away for four. Much-needed boundary for England.

    Over 2 Aamir 0-W-0-0-1-0 10-1 WICKET! Great start for Pakistan and teenage fast bowler Mohammad Aamir. He gets away with a wide first ball, and then does even more than get away with a wide half-volley as Bopara slashes the ball to backward point where Shoaib Malike clings on to a fine catch going to his left. Bopara would hit that ball for four nine times out of 10, but this time he's picked out the fielder. That brings KP to the crease very early in the piece, and he clips his first ball to square-leg. No single though. The wide half-volley appears to be Aamir's danger ball, as he bowls another one that Pietersen miscues to midwicket. No run again. Pietersen gives the Achilles a test with a quick single to cover and gets home easily. A wide and a bunch of half-volleys from Aamir. The result: one for one. Only against England. Sensational start for the youngster.

    Over 1 Arafat 0-0-4-1-0-4 9-0 - Huge roar as Arafat bustles in, and he gets a bit of outswing with his first delivery. Bopara lets it through to Kamran Akmal. The next ball is left again, and Pakistan are on the attack with two slips in place. Bopara's on the attack too, though, as he crashes the third ball of the game over point and just short of a maximum. More huge cheers - from the England fans this time. The Oval is absolutely jumping. Bopara takes a dodgy single from the next ball and gets the benefit of the doubt from the third umpire after Shahid Afridi's direct hit diving in from cover. Very, very close. Wright is beaten by the next one, and again the Pakistan fans roar their approval. Arafat offers width again to end the over, and Wright laces it through cover-point for four. Still pace in this pitch, and it's still a very good surface to bat on. England will need plenty - especially if they plan to bowl and field like they did the other night.

    HERE WE GO The only England players to emerge from Friday with dignity intact, Luke Wright and Ravi Bopara, are out in the middle to open the batting. Yasir Arafat has the new ball.

    STAND PLEASE The players are out for the national anthems. Cracking atmosphere at The Oval, with both anthems doing the job of getting the fans going. Both teams have plenty of support here today.

    TEAM NEWS Some surprising news from Pakistan as well: Sohail Tanvir sits this game out. They line up as follows: Salman Butt, Ahmad Shahzad, Kamran Akmal, Younus Khan, Misbah-ul-Haq, Shoaib Malik, Shahid Afridi, Yasir Arafat, Umar Gul, Mohammad Amir, Saeed Ajmal

    TEAM NEWS Pietersen plays, while Dimi Mascarenhas and Graeme Swann are also in the side. Eoin Morgan and Ryan Sidebottom sit out so it's two spinners and - controversially - a player capable of hitting sixes in the middle-order. Dangerous modern thinking from the selectors there.

    TOSS Bad start for England. Younus Khan wins the toss and chooses to bowl first. "It seems chasing is the easier thing," says Younus, quite correctly. There also could be some weather around and the D/L method usually favours the side chasing. As long as they're not coached by John Dyson.

    1705 "I think that Napier should be called up for today's game because he deserves a chance and also if they take all of our best players at Essex they might as well use them!" says Harry King. Let me know your thoughts by emailing Dave.Tickner@bskyb.com

    1655 In interview with Nasser Hussain, Ravi Bopara says Kevin Pietersen is "pretty fit today" and he expects him to play. Presumably Rob Key will make way, but there's every chance that won't be the only change from Orange Friday. Pietersen appeared to be taking a painkiller on the outfield earlier, which suggests he's probably going to give it a go. Wait for the stink kicked up if he misses the Ashes...

    1620 England have at least avoided the ignominy of being the first side eliminated from the tournament after Scotland bowed out with defeat at the hands of South Africa. Cracking catch from Kyle Coetzer for Scotland though, and the Durham batstman then showed far more initiative than most of his England rivals.

    1540 Still nearly two hours until the off here at The Oval, but the KP circus rumbles on. He'll have a late fitness test to decide whether he'll be able to take part in this must-win match for England against Group B top seeds Pakistan.
