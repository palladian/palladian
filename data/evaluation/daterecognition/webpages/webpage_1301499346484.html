EMERGING MARKETS-Brazil's currency firms, defying cenbank

Published September 09, 2010

* Yields on Brazil rates fall on inflation slowdown (Updates prices)

By Samantha Pearson and Jean Luis Arce

SAO PAULO/MEXICO CITY (Reuters) - Brazil's realfirmed Thursday, defying attempts by the country's centralbank to weaken the currency and protect local exporters.

Euphoria over booming foreign investment in Latin America'sbiggest economy has recently boosted the real, lifting it lastweek to its strongest level against the U.S. dollar this year.

After seesawing Thursday, the real closed 0.12percent stronger at 1.721 per U.S. dollar on the local spotmarket. It was boosted by a rally among the region's currenciesfollowing stronger U.S. jobs data that increased appetite forriskier assets.

Investors boosted the real despite the decision by Brazil'scentral bank to step up its intervention in the market bothWednesday and Thursday by calling more than one auction to buyU.S. dollars.

Traders expect a massive share offering by state-owned oilcompany Petrobras, planned for later this month, to lure $15billion to $25 billion of foreign investment.

The capitalization plan will not be the only deal to boostthe real, Marjorie Hernandez, a currency strategist at HSBC inNew York, said in a note to clients.

"The fulfillment of Petrobras' capitalization will alsounclog the domestic capital markets pipeline, opening the doorfor other M&A deals in the latter part of the year," she said.

The bank changed its forecast for the Brazilian real to1.70 in the third quarter and 1.74 in the fourth quarter, froma previous forecast of 1.84 for both quarters.

Elsewhere in the region, the Mexican peso strengthened 0.48 percent to 12.9900 per dollar after new U.S.claims for unemployment benefits fell more than expected lastweek to their lowest level in two months.

Mexico's fragile growth is dependent on the economicrecovery of the United States since the country sends about 80percent of its exports over its northern border.

"This good data got rid of the bad sentiment which has beenaccumulating in the last month and this is supporting thepeso," said Ramon Cordova, a trader at brokerage BASE inMonterrey, Mexico.

The Chilean peso firmed 0.1 percent to 496.2 perdollar. The country's central bank is expected to raise itsbenchmark interest rate by 50 basis points to 2.5 percent atits Sept. 16 monetary policy meeting.

SLOWER BRAZIL INFLATION SURPRISES AGAIN

Yields fell on Brazilian interest rate futures following data showing slower-than-expected inflation forAugust.

Investors bet the data would put less pressure on thecountry's central bank to raise the cost of borrowing overcoming months.

At the beginning of this month, Brazil's central bank heldits benchmark interest rate at 10.75 percent, ending a monetarytightening cycle that helped cool a red-hot economy.

The central bank also released the minutes from thatmeeting Thursday, showing it is not currently concernedabout a possible sharp rise in the cost of consumer goods.

However, some economists believe that rising food pricesnext year will force the bank to revise its view and increaseborrowing costs again.

The yield on the contract due January 2012, one ofthe most heavily traded contracts of the early session, fell to11.27 percent from 11.36 percent. (Editing by Dan Grebler)
