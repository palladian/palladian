Too soon to make Powderfinger concert decision

BY DELLARAM JAMALI
14 Sep, 2010 12:02 AM
DESPITE wet weather concerns, Powderfinger will not make a decision regarding an alternate venue for their upcoming Ballarat concert until closer to the date.

The statement comes after doubts were raised about their October 16 performance at Lake Wendouree's North Gardens due to wet weather conditions.

But Powderfinger manager Paul Piticco said it was too early to make a final decision.

"We have been warned by the venue that there are drainage issues in light of the rain and floods currently hitting the area," he said.

"We anticipate that the venue will dry out if the rain lets up and with the show still over a month away, we will not be making any decisions until closer to the show date."

The Courier believes that Ballarat City Council may be considering the Ballarat Showgrounds as an alternate venue should the need arise.

Powderfinger will be supported by Jet and Paul Dempsey for the sold-out show.

Their concert is part of their nation-wide Sunsets tour with their last-ever performance being in Brisbane in November.
