M1 covers new ground with world famous Cirque du Soleil

SINGAPORE,1 July 1999 - M1, Singapore's newest mobile and paging service provider, underscored its vision to break new ground when it announced today that it will commit over S$1 million to bring SaltimbancoTM, the captivating Cirque du Soleil production, to Singapore audiences.

Saltimbanco will premiere on 25th November on the Padang, as part of Singapore's MillenniaMania celebrations.

In a signing ceremony at the Au Jardin restaurant in Botanic Gardens, M1 and Cirque du Soleil sealed the partnership in a style befitting two businesses which have grown from strength to strength by being bold and innovative.

M1's Chief Executive Officer, Neil Montefiore, and Cirque du Soleil's Managing Director, Helene Larivee, with the help of vibrant coloured paint, palmpainted a giant-sized agreement document specially prepared for the occasion.

M1 customers will benefit from this latest partnership, with privileged bookings on show tickets, discounts on mobile phones and accessories in conjunction with the shows, souvenirs, exclusive M1 customer show nights, and prizes of mobile phones, air tickets and tickets to other Cirque du Soleil shows around the world.

Cirque du Soleil combines a novel blend of circus arts, street performance, spectacular costumes, spellbinding music and magical lighting. It is a circus that does not feature animals, just sheer human energy, leading Time magazine to describe it as "the greatest show on earth".

It was Cirque du Soleil's 'dare-to-be-different' approach that clinched M1's decision to play a key role in the Singapore leg of its Asia Pacific circuit.

That, and the fact that they have a common icon, the sun. It was perhaps inevitable that M1's brand concept of 'Everywhere Under The Sun' found a natural ally in Cirque du Soleil which means 'Circus of the Sun' in French. The pairing will be particularly apt as the sun rises on the Millennium.

Discussing the partnership, Montefiore said that while M1 and Cirque du Soleil are distinctly different businesses, "our brand personalities and values are strikingly similar. Both are bright, young and growing brands which have set out to be different and innovative. And we are both making a huge impact in our respective businesses which have long been dominated by bigger players.

"Just as Cirque du Soleil has transformed the concept of the circus as we know it, we believe M1 has given the deregulated telecoms market in Singapore an entirely fresh perspective. In the short period since its launch, M1 has offered consumers new and exciting options as an alternative mobile service provider."

Cirque du Soleil's Larivee said its alliance with M1 will ensure Saltimbanco reaches a broad segment of Singaporeans through M1's strong customer base. "It will certainly boost our own $1 million marketing programme of intensive above and below-the-line activities, including public relations, advertising and consumer promotions within and beyond Singapore."

The show, which is currently in Australia, has received enthusiastic reviews with additional shows staged to meet demand. Following the Singapore run, Saltimbanco will be performed in Hong Kong and Japan.

About M1

M1, Singapore's newest mobile and paging service provider, is a consortium of the Keppel Group, Singapore Press Holdings, Cable & Wireless and Cable & Wireless HKT.

M1 has been a keen supporter of the arts in Singapore, the most recent initiative being the M1 Youth Connection, presented by the Necessary Stage. The company has also supported several other events such as Ballet Under The Stars by the Singapore Dance Theatre, fringe activities at the Singapore Arts Festival and the National Education Exhibition. It has supported the Singapore Repertory Theatre the last two years.

About Cirque du Soleil

Since its beginnings in 1984, Cirque du Soleil has mesmerized over 18 million people around the world.

What began as a small group of travelling performers carting their modest circus paraphernalia from town to town in Canada, is now a global organisation with nearly 1,500 employees. Founded by Guy Laliberti, the company's studio and international headquarters is in Montreal.

Saltimbanco is one of three shows that is currently on tour. The other two are Quidam which is touring Europe, and Dralion which is on its North American tour.

In addition to taking its shows on tour around the world, Cirque du Soleil also has several permanent productions:

  • Mystere at the Treasure Island Hotel in Las Vegas since 1993;
  • "O", an aquatic show at Bellagio, Las Vegas which has been billed as "A Show that Soars - and Swims" by TIME magazine.
  • La Nouba, a brand new resident show and the company's very first merchandise outlet in Downtown Disney's West Side near Orlando, Florida and
  • Alegrma at Beau Rivage, a new resort in Biloxi, Mississippi, U.S.A..

Cirque du Soleil generated C$150 million (S$177 million) in sales in 1997 and C$190 million (S$224 million) in 1998 and expects this figure to grow to C$320 million (S$377 million) in 1999.
