Hyderabad residents stock up as curfew is relaxed

Vegetable markets and general stores see huge rush of customers as city limps back to normality

  • By Omer Farooq, Correspondent
  • Published: 00:00 April 2, 2010

Hyderabad:  For the first time since it was imposed on Monday, curfew in the riot-hit old city of Hyderabad was relaxed for two hours yesterday morning.

Commissioner Abdul Khayyum Khan told the media that there were no untoward incidents during the period in any of the 17 police station limits.

There was a huge rush of people at the Mir Alam Mandi, other vegetable markets and general stores to buy essential commodities. As only women and elderly people were allowed, there was a long queue of women at shops as well as at the vans selling vegetables and milk.

State Home Minister Sabita Indra Reddy said 28 truck-loads of vegetables and 45,000 litres of milk were sold during the two-hour period.

But people were angry that some traders had exploited the situation.

"Some of them were selling vegetables at double and triple price. Even milk was sold for more than Rs50 (Dh4.08) a litre. There was no official to deal with the problem," one woman complained at Dabeerpura.

Tomato was being sold for Rs30 per kg at some places.

Arrests

Reddy said four unscrupulous traders were arrested while others were given a stern warning.

People were also unhappy that police allowed only women and elderly people to come out.

"What if there was no elderly man in our home? I alone could come out. Tell me where I should go. Should I buy vegetables or go for medicine? asked another woman at Qazipura.

The commissioner made it clear that curfew will not be relaxed today.

Khan said police did every thing possible to reduce the hardship of people during the three days of curfew.

"Removing it completely was not possible," he said.

While middle class families could manage to take home whatever they could, it was the poor who found it difficult as they were not able to earn anything for four days.

The minister said 21 companies of paramilitary forces had been deployed and that another 10 companies were being brought. "Situation is under control and it is improving," she said.

Wanted politicians

Hyderabad city is looking for two local politicians — Telugu Desam Party's Raju Singh and Bharatiya Janata Party's Vaikuntam — in connection with the communal riots in the city.

They are among the several people who have emerged as prime suspects during the probe by the Special Investigations Team into the violence that broke out last Saturday.

The Commissioner's Task Force raided the residences of both the politicians, but they managed to escape. While police was searching for them, Raju Singh spoke to local television channels twice, alleging that he and his supporters were being targeted unnecessarily.

"It is the Congress and the MIM [Majlis-e-Ittehadul Muslimeen] who are responsible for violence", he said. "We are being targeted because we defended Hindus."

Singh, a member of Hindu Vahini and other similar organisations, was involved in the first incident of violence at Musa Bowli on Saturday night. Police say he also played a major role in spreading the trouble to other areas. Singh was prime accused in the abduction and murder of two Christian pastors in 2003 in Tappa Chabutra area of Hyderabad. Police say he organised and led the mobs from Purana Pul area in the current trouble.

Vaikuntam on the other hand was suspected of being involved in the trouble in Begum Bazar area during Bajrang Dal's procession on Tuesday, when violence spread to new city areas and curfew had to be imposed.
