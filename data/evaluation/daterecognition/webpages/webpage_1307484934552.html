Ethics complaint alleges Cagle affair

  • Print
  • E-mail

By Aaron Gould Sheinin

The Atlanta Journal-Constitution

A man who has endorsed Lt. Gov. Casey Cagle's opponent in Tuesday's election filed a state ethics complaint Friday alleging that Cagle had an affair with a campaign staffer and paid her nearly $200,000 in campaign funds.

The complaint was filed by Ray Boyd, a businessman who briefly ran for governor as a Republican and then as an independent this summer. He has publicly endorsed Democrat Carol Porter, whom Cagle faces Tuesday.

Boyd's complaint offers no proof of the affair, which has been a Capitol rumor for years, and allegedly occurred around the time Cagle, then a state senator, was elected lieutenant governor in 2006.

Boyd alleges that Cagle violated state ethics laws by paying the staffer above market rate for her professional services, while the law requires campaign funds be used "to defray ordinary and necessary expenses ... incurred in connection with such candidate's campaign for elective office or such public officer's fulfillment or retention of such office."

Neither Boyd nor Cagle could not be reached for comment Friday. When asked about the rumors in the past, Cagle refused to answer. His campaign released a statement denying the allegations.

“Mr. Boyd’s allegation is absolutely false and deliberately hurtful to the lieutenant governor, his family and the staff who work for him," Ryan Cassin, Cagle's campaign manager, said in a statement. "Carol Porter obviously wants someone else to spread lies on her behalf, and her willingness to spoon feed poison to an attention-seeking millionaire is despicable and pathetic. We expect Carol’s dirty tricks team to continue these kinds of attacks over the next several days in a desperate attempt to erase a 20-point deficit.”

The woman with whom Cagle allegedly had the affair also denied Boyd's claims.

“Mr. Boyd’sÂ accusations are appalling and insulting," she said in a statement Friday. "There is no truth to any of this whatsoever. As a private citizen, I’m shocked that he and others would bring me into their failing and desperate campaign.”

Porter's campaign said late Friday she was not responsible for Boyd's actions.

"Casey Cagle’s attacks are a false and desperate attempt to deflect from the situation," Porter said in a statement. "We had no knowledge of Mr. Boyd’s ethics complaint until called by Lori Geary from WSB-TV. Mr. Boyd seems to be fed up with the corruption and as [University of Georgia political scientist] Charles Bullock has coined it, ‘the fraternity boy antics' under the Gold Dome."

Boyd alleges that a Capitol secretary walked in on Cagle and the woman engaged in a sex act while in his office. The secretary released a statement through her attorney, state Sen. Seth Harp, R-Midland.

The statement alleged that Boyd had sought to induce the woman to lie about Cagle. It said Boyd offered the woman high-paying jobs and other benefits "in exchange for false testimony about events that never happened and I have denied dozens of times."
